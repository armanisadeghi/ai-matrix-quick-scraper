<h1 id="section-title">
        What types of breast implants are available?
    </h1>
<h2>Saline breast implants</h2><p><strong>Saline breast implants</strong> are filled with sterile salt water. Should the implant shell leak, a saline implant will collapse and the saline will be absorbed and naturally expelled by the body.</p><p>Saline breast implants provide a uniform shape, firmness and feel, and are FDA-approved for augmentation in women age 18 or older.</p><h2>Structured saline breast implants</h2><p><strong>Structured implants</strong> are filled with sterile salt water, and contain an inner structure which aims to make the implant feel more natural.</p><h2>Silicone breast implants</h2><p><strong>Silicone breast implants</strong> are filled with silicone gel. The gel feels a bit more like natural breast tissue. If the implant leaks, the gel may remain within the implant shell, or may escape into the breast implant pocket. A leaking implant filled with silicone gel will not collapse.</p><p>If you choose silicone implants, you may need to visit your plastic surgeon regularly to make sure the implants are functioning properly. An ultrasound or MRI screening can assess the condition of breast implants.</p><p>Silicone breast implants are FDA-approved for augmentation in women age 22 or older.</p><h2>Gummy bear breast implants</h2><p>Form-stable implants are sometimes referred to as <strong>gummy bear breast implants</strong> because they maintain their shape even when the implant shell is broken.</p><p>The consistency of the silicone gel inside the implant is thicker than traditional silicone gel implants. These implants are also firmer than traditional implants.</p><p>Shaped gummy bear breast implants have more projection at the bottom and are tapered towards the top. If a shaped implant rotates, it may lead to an unusual appearance of the breast that requires a separate procedure to correct.</p><p>Placement of gummy bear implants requires a slightly longer incision in the skin.</p><h2>Round breast implants</h2><p><strong>Round breast implants</strong> have a tendency to make breasts appear fuller than form-stable implants. Higher profile options can achieve even more projection.</p><p>Because round implants are the same shape all over, there is less concern about them rotating out of place.</p><h2>Smooth breast implants</h2><p><strong>Smooth breast implants</strong> are the softest feeling. They can move with the breast implant pocket, which may give more natural movement.</p><p>Smooth implants may have some palpable or visible rippling under the skin.</p><h2>Textured breast implants</h2><p><strong>Textured breast implants</strong> develop scar tissue to stick to the implant, making them less likely to move around inside of the breast and become repositioned.</p><p>Texturing offers some advantage in diminishing the risk of a tight scar capsule.</p><p><em>*It's important to note that breast implant-associated anaplastic large cell lymphoma (BIA-ALCL) occurs most frequently in patients who have breast implants with textured surfaces. For more information on BIA-ALCL, visit our <a href="/patient-safety/breast-implant-safety/bia-alcl-summary">BIA-ALCL Summary page</a>. Discuss all benefits and risks related to your breast implant procedure with your board-certified plastic surgeon. Understanding all potential risk factors will help with better decision-making that is best for you and your health.</em></p><p><a class="btn btn-primary" href="/patient-safety/breast-implant-safety/allergan-biocell-device-withdrawal" title="Allergan Biocell Device Withdrawal Information"><span class="glyphicon glyphicon-warning-sign"></span>  Allergan Biocell Device Withdrawal Information</a></p><p>Implant manufacturers occasionally introduce new styles and types of breast implants, so there may be additional options available.</p><p>Whether you choose saline or silicone implants, it is important for you to monitor your breast implants and follow-up with your plastic surgeon for appropriate checkups.</p> <div class="row">
